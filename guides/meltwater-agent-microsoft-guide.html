Onboarding customers to the Meltwater Agent for Microsoft

A practical reference for IMs — from confirming eligibility to getting the agent live in a customer's Microsoft Copilot environment, with or without a Meltwater license.

Background

What the Meltwater Agent for Microsoft does

It's the same underlying capability as Mira Studio, surfaced inside the customer's own Microsoft Copilot environment instead of the Meltwater platform. Customers ask questions in plain English and get Meltwater data back — without logging into Meltwater at all.

💬

Conversational access

Available in Copilot Chat and Copilot in Teams today. Word, Excel, and PowerPoint shipped Q4 2025; Outlook is planned for Q1 2026.

📄

Deliverables, not just answers

Drafts media briefs, journalist pitches, and campaign summaries directly in Word and PowerPoint — exec-ready output in minutes, not hours.

🔓

Reaches beyond Meltwater licenses

The single biggest differentiator. Admins can add "Agent-only" users who never touch the Meltwater platform — extending value across the org without buying more seats.

🛡️

Trusted and compliant

Microsoft 365 certified, plus ISO 42001, 27001, and 27701. Customer data isn't used to train Meltwater or OpenAI models, and inputs are retained for a maximum of 30 days.

Before anything else

Eligibility check

Two requirements have to be true before this is even possible for a customer. Confirm both before you go any further.

1

Paid Microsoft Copilot license

Third-party agents — including Meltwater — are only available in the paid version of Microsoft Copilot. A free or trial Copilot tier won't support this. Confirm with the customer which Copilot tier they're on before promising anything.

2

Meltwater entitlement on contract

The account needs the meltwater-copilot and external-users entitlements. This is a PLI — check the contract, don't assume.

For net new customers, this is automatic. As long as meltwater-copilot and external-users are on the contract, entitlements are applied without any ticket or manual step from you. Just confirm both are actually on the PLI before you move forward.

Once eligibility is confirmed, figure out where the customer stands on awareness and intent. Both paths converge at IT approval — that step doesn't get skipped either way.

✅

They already know they want it

No education needed. Skip straight to confirming IT readiness — don't jump to activation until that's locked down.

Signals: customer asked about it directly, they're already Copilot power users, a technical or IT stakeholder raised it first.

↓ Jump to IT approval
💡

They're eligible but haven't connected the dots

Start with discovery to understand their workflow, then frame the value — especially the reach-without-a-license angle. Then move to IT approval.

Signals: customer hasn't mentioned Copilot or the Agent, they're newer to Meltwater, PLI is on the contract but nobody's brought it up.

Path B · Step 1

Discovery & framing

Ask these before pitching anything. The goal is to find a real, specific workflow the Agent can shorten — then frame the value around it.

1
What information are you expected to deliver regularly to your team or leadership, and why does it matter?
2
What types of requests or questions do you frequently get from colleagues, and how do you typically respond?
3
Which recurring requests do you wish you could handle faster or more efficiently?
4
What routine tasks take longer than they should — and what's slowing them down?
Talk track — leading with the reach angle

"One thing worth knowing: this isn't limited to people who already have a Meltwater login. We can add colleagues — across comms, marketing, even leadership — as Agent-only users inside Teams and Copilot, so they can ask questions and get Meltwater data without ever logging into the platform."

"That means the value isn't capped by your seat count. If there's a recurring request that lands on your desk from someone outside your core team, this is a way to let them self-serve instead of routing through you."

Both paths converge here

IT approval & readiness

This is the most common blocker, and it should be resolved before any activation work starts — not discovered after. Ideally this happens during the sales cycle, but in practice it often lands on you.

Quick diagnostic: have the customer search "Meltwater" in the Teams app store. If they see an Add button, no approval is needed. If they see Request — or don't see Meltwater at all — their IT or Security team has to approve it first.

Find the right person early

Listen in discovery for: "we need to check with IT," "our Teams is locked down," "procurement will loop in security." Ask directly: who manages Teams app approvals? What's your internal IT/security approval process? When does IT typically get involved?

Most likely — collaboration & IT
  • Microsoft 365 / O365 Administrator
  • Teams Administrator
  • IT Manager / Senior IT Manager
  • Digital Workplace Lead
Often involved — security & compliance
  • Information Security (IS) Manager
  • Cybersecurity Manager / Lead
  • IT Risk Manager
  • Data Privacy / Compliance Officer
"Digital Workplace" "Teams" or "O365" in title "Collaboration" "Security"

What to lead with

These are the facts that move IT/Security conversations fastest. Lead with credentials, then data handling, then control.

  • Microsoft 365 Certified — officially reviewed by Microsoft against industry security, compliance, and data-handling standards.
  • ISO 42001, ISO 27001, ISO 27701 — AI management, information security, and data privacy certifications.
  • No broad tenant access — Meltwater doesn't gain wide access to the customer's Microsoft tenant.
  • Minimal data processed — user ID, tenant ID, prompt, and structured results only. No sensitive data required.
  • 30-day retention — with the option for earlier deletion. Inputs are never used to train Meltwater or OpenAI models.
  • Full admin control — IT can enable or disable the Agent at any time, and scope access by user, group, or role in the Microsoft admin centers.
If the app is blocked: the customer's Teams/M365 administrator needs to go to Teams Admin Center → Teams apps → Permission policies, confirm third-party apps are allowed, and explicitly allow Meltwater by name or App ID.
Sending the Agent Compliance & Security Info Pack alongside the access request gives IT everything above in writing — attach it whenever a customer's IT team needs to review before approving.
Final step · Both paths

Activation & phased rollout

Because support for this product is digital-first and self-serve, a phased rollout matters more here than with most products. Agent-only users won't have context on Meltwater or the partnership unless you build that in deliberately.

Support model to know before you start: Agent-only users don't have direct access to Meltwater's support team or ticketing system. If they hit an issue, they go to their internal champion — a core Meltwater platform user — who can escalate through normal support channels if needed. This applies to every account, regardless of size.
1

IT approval confirmed

Don't proceed past this point until the customer's IT team has approved the Teams app installation. See the IT Approval section above if this isn't done yet.

2

Access control — core team only, first

Grant Agent access only to users who already have Meltwater platform access. Don't open this up to the broader org yet — that comes later, deliberately.

Before assigning anyone, confirm how many Agent seats the customer purchased. Every user added against the Agent — core team or Agent-only — counts against that number, so it's worth knowing the ceiling before you start handing out access.

3

Onboard the core team

Identify two roles within the customer's core team:

  • Admin — the main point of contact, a platform user responsible for the rollout and for supporting Agent-only users
  • Champions — platform users responsible for evangelizing the Agent within their teams and supporting Agent-only users

Train this group on the Agent's capabilities and intended use cases using the customer-facing onboarding deck.

4

How access actually gets granted

This is the customer Admin's job, not yours — but you should know the mechanics well enough to walk them through it the first time. A Meltwater subscription has three user/seat types based on which applications are selected: Meltwater only (platform seat), Teams only (this is the Agent-only seat — officially "Meltwater Agent for Microsoft"), or both (one seat each).

The Admin adds each user from Account → Manage Users → Add User (top right):

  • First name, last name, email address
  • Check Send email invite — required, the user won't get setup instructions without it
  • Time zone, and language (note: the Agent is optimized for English only)
  • Workspaces is optional and not currently integrated with the Agent — skip it for Agent-only users
  • Under Applications, select Meltwater, Teams, or both. Permissions are only assignable for users with platform access — that section disables itself if only Teams is selected

What the user receives depends on selection: Meltwater-only and Meltwater + Teams users both get a single email invite to set up their Meltwater login. Teams-only (Agent-only) users get an email invite to set up their Meltwater login plus step-by-step instructions for installing the Agent for Microsoft app — that login is required even though they'll never touch the core platform.

📋 You'll need a list from the customer. For anyone beyond the core team, the Admin needs a first name, last name, and email per person before they can add them — get that list from your Champions before the planned rollout date, not on the day of.

To manage existing Agent users — resend a pending invite, edit access, or remove someone — the Admin goes to Manage Users → External Users, not the main Users tab. Pencil icon edits access, trashcan removes it, mail icon resends an invite stuck on Pending. Full walkthrough with screenshots is in Adding & Managing Agent Users on mCommunity.

5

Plan the rollout to Agent-only users

Work with the core team to set a rollout date for broader distribution, and have them collect the name/email list for everyone going on as Agent-only ahead of that date. Don't let this drift — an undefined rollout date is the most common reason this stalls after the core team is trained.

6

Internal communication

The customer's core team — not Meltwater — drafts and sends the announcement to Agent-only users. It should explain the Meltwater partnership, what the Agent is, what to expect, and who to contact internally for support. A communication template is available for them to use.

⏱ Ideally this communication goes out the same day Agent-only users are added — don't let there be a gap between access and explanation.
7

Roll out to Agent-only users

Using the list collected in Step 5, the Admin adds each person via Manage Users → Add User with only the Teams application selected. Each receives the two-part invite — Meltwater login setup plus Agent for Microsoft install instructions — covered in Step 4 above.

Product training

Prompt strategy & what the Agent can see

Once users are in, the quality of what they get out depends entirely on how they ask. Use this to set expectations and teach good prompting in the same conversation.

Where it lives: in Copilot Chat, type @meltwater to reference the Agent directly. In Teams or Office apps, select All Agents → Meltwater from the left-hand navigation. Conversations persist across Copilot and Office app instances.

Writing prompts with TRACI

T
Task
What you want the Agent to accomplish
R
Role
The role the Agent should assume
A
Audience
Who the output is for
C
Context
Relevant background information
I
Instructions
Formatting or specific guidelines
Example — building a media pitch with TRACI

Task: Write a media pitch. Role: PR specialist. Audience: a busy, oversaturated tech journalist. Context: a new AI tool that reduces PR workload by 30%. Instructions: professional tone, highlight key benefits, end with a call to action.

"Write a persuasive media pitch as a PR specialist to a busy journalist who is oversaturated with generic media outreach, convincing them to adopt a new software tool that reduces a PR professional's workload by 30%. Use professional language, highlight the key benefits, and include a clear call to action at the end."

What the Agent can and can't see

Set this expectation early so customers aren't surprised mid-prompt.

✓ Has access to
  • The same data (including add-ons) in the customer's Meltwater account
  • Broadcast, podcast, print, and online partnership sources
  • Saved searches in Meltwater Explore
  • Social listening for Facebook, Instagram, Threads, and TikTok — if authenticated and tracked in Explore
✗ Does not have access to
  • Workspace-level data — account-level only
  • Premium (paywalled) or regionally restricted content
  • Author lists or media lists
  • Owned and paid connection data
  • Content added via RSS feeds, the Chrome Extension, or the Added Content page
Language support: currently English only. The Agent generally responds in the language a question was asked in, but behavior for non-English queries isn't guaranteed yet — enhanced support is planned.
